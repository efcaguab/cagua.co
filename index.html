What I've Done

Work

I designed a workshop for environmental scientists to implement good data collection and management practices to speed up decision making in conservation (2017).

I ran a series of seminars to train biologists at the University of Canterbury in the use of HPC to analyse genetic and environmental big data (2017-18).

I improved species monitoring practices in Aldabra, a UNESCO World Heiritage Site, by mining large datasets collected by the Seychelles Island Foundation (2016).

I discovered that the largest fish in the world, which was previously though to be a highly migratory species, can, in fact, be a year-round resident (2014).

I found out how much is whale shark tourism worth. The results supported the creation of a management plan for the largest protected area in the Maldives (2013).

I designed a system that uses artificial neural networks and underwater recording stations to detect ilegal dynamite fishing in Tanzania.(2013).
